Research Excellence & Innovation

Siddharth Goswami is an emerging researcher in Biomedical Informatics and Computational Biology, recognized for integrating artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and systems biology toward advancing precision medicine and translational biomedicine.

As a first-author of 20+ research papers published in prestigious venues including IEEE, Elsevier, Springer Nature, and IGI Global, his work spans AI-driven healthcare, computational drug design, and systems-level biology. He is the first inventor of a published patent in Alzheimer's drug discovery and author of an accepted academic book titled Comprehensive Biomedical Informatics (Bentham Science).

Currently working under Prof. Sachin Sharma (Stanford University Top 2% Scientist), Siddharth's research contributions include the development of reinforcement learning models for clinical risk prediction, quantum computing pipelines for NGS-based personalized medicine, and deep learning approaches for molecular drug design.

With 100+ citations, an h-index of 5+, and ranking in the top 37% globally (AD Scientific Index 2025), Siddharth is dedicated to leveraging computational modeling to accelerate discoveries in drug development, clinical decision support, and biomedical data science.

Published Patent

Innovation in Alzheimer's Disease Drug Discovery

Novel Ligand Molecules Targeting GSK-3β Enzyme for the Treatment of Alzheimer's Disease

Inventors:

Goswami, S. (First Inventor), Singh, P., & Yadav, B.

Application Number:

202411101941 A

Filing Date:

December 23, 2024

Publication Date:

January 3, 2025

Journal Reference:

The Patent Office Journal No. 1/2025, Page 735

This groundbreaking patent describes novel ligand molecules designed to target the GSK-3β enzyme, a key therapeutic target in Alzheimer's disease. Through computational drug repurposing and molecular dynamics simulations, these ligands demonstrate superior binding affinity and favorable pharmacokinetic properties including blood-brain barrier permeability. The innovation represents a significant advancement in the development of potential disease-modifying therapies for Alzheimer's disease, addressing a critical unmet medical need in neurodegenerative medicine.
